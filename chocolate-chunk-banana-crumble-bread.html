Chocolate Chunk Banana Crumble Bread

Picking the Right Bananas

Dozens of banana breads later, I have finally perfected my banana picking skills. This receipe does not have a lot of added sugar (sometimes I leave the sugar out entirely), so the key to still having a sweet and flavourful banana bread is really really ripe bananas.

Steps for the perfect baking bananas

  1. Ripen Bananas at Room Temperture until VERY Spotty (reduced to clear bananas are ususally perfect)
  2. Put Bananas in the freezer overnight (or longer, I've stored mine for up to 3 months and they did not lose flavour)
  3. Defrost Bananas in Fridge overnight or at room temperture for at least 5 hours (bananas should be fully defrosted, even leaking slightly)

Ingredients

Banana Bread

  • 300g almond flour
  • 80g ground flax seeds
  • 5 bananas (see above)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 100g 70% dark chocolate bar
  • 60g brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract (you can add less, I really like vanilla extract)
  • 1-2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt

Crumble Topping

  • 50g salted butter (cold)
  • 60g brown sugar
  • 90g quick oats
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt

Directions

  1. Preheat Oven to 350F
  2. Roughly chop dark chocolate bar until you have small chunks. I like to keep mine about the size of a hazelnut but you decide how small you want to chop them.
  3. Mix wet ingredients together: Bananas, Eggs, Vanilla Extract, Brown Sugar.
  4. Mix dry ingredients: Almond Flour, Ground Flax Seeds, Baking Powder, Cinnamon, Salt.
  5. Combine wet and dry ingredients until you have a uniform batter.
  6. Fold in chocolate chunks.
  7. Line baking dish with butter and pour in dough. Tap on counter a few times to reduce air pockets.
  8. Combine crumble ingredient in a bowl. Use your fingers to massage the cold butter into the sugar and oats. Stop when there are no more large butter chunks.
  9. Sprinkle crumble on top of the banana bread
  10. Bake for 30-35 minutes.
  11. Let cool for about 15 minutes and enjoy!
  12. I usually store mine in the fridge and reheat in the microwave for about 40 seconds. It should be good for at least 3-4 days, if it lasts that long!
